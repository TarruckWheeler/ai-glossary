🎯 General AI Concepts

🟢

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Computer systems that can think and learn like humans. AI can understand language, recognize images, make decisions, and solve problems - basically doing tasks that usually need human intelligence.
🟢

Narrow AI (Weak AI)

AI that's really good at one specific thing. Like a chess-playing program or spam filter - it excels at its job but can't do anything else. This is all the AI we have today.
🟣

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

Hypothetical AI that could match human intelligence across all areas. An AGI could learn new tasks, understand context, and think creatively like humans. This doesn't exist yet - it's a future goal.
🟢

Turing Test

A test to see if AI can fool humans into thinking it's human. If you chat with an AI and can't tell it's not a person, it passes the test. Named after computer scientist Alan Turing.
🟢

Augmented Intelligence

AI designed to help humans, not replace them. Like a smart calculator that helps you solve math problems faster, or a spell-checker that helps you write better.

🧠 Machine Learning

🟢

Machine Learning (ML)

Teaching computers to learn from examples instead of programming every rule. Like showing a computer thousands of cat photos so it can recognize cats in new pictures.
🔵

Supervised Learning

Learning with a teacher who shows correct answers. You show the AI examples with labels (like "this is a cat, this is a dog") so it learns to identify them correctly.
🔵

Unsupervised Learning

AI learning patterns from data without being told what to look for. Like finding hidden groups in customer data or discovering topics in text without knowing what topics exist.
🔵

Deep Learning

AI that uses artificial "brain networks" with many layers. Each layer learns different features - like edges, then shapes, then full objects. Powers most modern AI breakthroughs.
🔵

Neural Network

Computer system inspired by how brain neurons connect. Artificial neurons process information and pass it to the next layer, learning patterns through training.
🟣

Overfitting

When AI memorizes training examples too well and fails on new data. Like a student who memorizes practice tests but fails the real exam because they didn't truly understand the concepts.

💬 Natural Language Processing

🟢

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Teaching computers to understand and use human language. Enables chatbots, translation, voice assistants, and text analysis.
🔵

Large Language Model (LLM)

Massive AI trained on billions of text examples. Can write, answer questions, and have conversations. Examples: GPT-4, Claude, Bard.
🟢

Chatbot

AI that can have conversations with humans. From simple customer service bots to advanced AI assistants like ChatGPT and Claude.
🟣

Prompt Engineering

The art of asking AI the right questions to get better answers. Like learning how to phrase requests to get exactly what you need from AI tools.
🟢

Hallucination

When AI confidently makes up false information. Like an AI claiming a fake fact or inventing a source that doesn't exist. Always verify important AI answers!
🔵

Transformer

The AI architecture that revolutionized language processing. Uses "attention" to understand which words are most important in context. Powers GPT, BERT, and most modern language AI.

👁️ Computer Vision

🟢

Computer Vision

Teaching computers to "see" and understand images and videos. Used in everything from photo tagging to medical diagnosis to self-driving cars.
🔵

Object Detection

AI that can find and identify multiple objects in an image. Not just "there's a cat" but "there's a cat at this location, a dog over there, and a car in the background."
🟢

Facial Recognition

AI that can identify people by their faces. Used in phone unlocking, photo tagging, and security systems. Raises privacy concerns.
